## Module 4 — Putting it into practice (map → toolkit → staying sharp)

### Lesson 4.1 — Mapping your AI use cases (lesson ID 47)
Turn understanding into a personal system. Start from your real week rather than from tools; run each task through the four-question filter (value, verifiability, stakes, skill-to-keep); sort tasks into Delegate / Assist / Keep-human; and define red-zone tasks (others' private data, high-stakes decisions, messages that must be genuinely yours) in advance.

### Lesson 4.2 — Building your AI toolkit (lesson ID 48)
Few tools, well used. The four categories most people need: a general assistant, a research tool, a writing aid, and one specialist. Choose tools by fit, trust, cost, and data practices. Turn repeated "Delegate" tasks into reusable prompt templates, and wrap everything in a draft → verify → decide workflow. Prune the kit periodically.

### Lesson 4.3 — Staying sharp (lesson ID 49) — final lesson
The course's capstone. The cognitive-offloading trade-off (callback to Module 3): effort is part of how we learn, so offload drudgery but keep desirable difficulty. Three principles: attempt before you outsource, verify actively (not passively), and keep core skills warm. A light weekly rhythm plus awareness of over-reliance warning signs keeps the mind sharp. Closes the course on: think first, verify always.
