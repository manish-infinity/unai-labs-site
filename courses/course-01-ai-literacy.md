## Course Overview

**Tagline:** Stop copying prompts you found online. Start understanding what you're actually doing.

**Description:**
AI tools are everywhere. But most people use them like they used Google in 1999 — typing things in and hoping for relevant results. This course changes that. In four weeks, you'll build a genuine understanding of how modern AI works, how to communicate with it effectively, and how to integrate it into your real work without losing your own judgment.

No coding required. No technical background needed.

**Outcomes — by the end of this course, you will:**
- Explain how large language models work at a conceptual level
- Write prompts that consistently get better results across any AI tool
- Recognize the 5 most common AI failure modes and catch them before they matter
- Build a simple AI-assisted workflow for your most time-consuming task
- Make an informed decision about when to use AI and when not to

**Duration:** 4 weeks | ~2 hours per week (8 hours total)
**Format:** Video lessons + written exercises
**Level:** Beginner — no prior AI or technical experience required

## Module 1: What Is This Thing, Really?
*Week 1 | 3 lessons + 1 exercise*

### Lesson 1.1: The Autocomplete That Changed Everything
- What language models actually do (predict the next token)
- Why this simple idea produces such surprising outputs
- The difference between intelligence and pattern completion

### Lesson 1.2: What AI Can Do, What It Can't, and What It'll Confidently Get Wrong
- The three things AI is genuinely good at
- The four things it fails at in predictable, documentable ways
- Hallucinations: why they happen and how to reduce them
- Exercise: test your AI tool on 5 factual questions and document where it goes wrong

### Lesson 1.3: The Landscape of AI Tools (2025-2026)
- ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot — what's the difference?
- How to choose the right tool for your use case
- Free vs. paid: when it matters

**Week 1 Exercise:**
Spend 30 minutes using any AI tool for a task you'd normally do yourself. Notice where it helps, where it's wrong, and where you're unsure. Write 3 observations.

## Module 2: The Art of the Prompt
*Week 2 | 4 lessons + 1 exercise*

### Lesson 2.1: Why Prompts Matter More Than the Model
- How the model "sees" your input
- The role of context, framing, and specificity

### Lesson 2.2: The Five Elements of a Good Prompt
- Role / Persona
- Task / Goal
- Context / Background
- Constraints / Format
- Example / Demonstration
- Practice: rewrite 3 weak prompts using this framework

### Lesson 2.3: Iterative Prompting — Treating AI Like a Conversation
- Why your first prompt is almost never your best one
- The revision loop: how to steer AI toward what you actually need
- Chain-of-thought prompting: getting AI to reason step by step

### Lesson 2.4: Prompt Patterns That Save Hours
- The "You are a..." persona pattern
- The "Let's think step by step" reasoning pattern
- The "Give me 5 options, then help me choose" decision pattern
- The "What am I missing?" reflection pattern

**Week 2 Exercise:**
Take your Week 1 task. Spend 45 minutes using the 5-element prompt framework. Document your best prompt and the result.

## Module 3: AI in Your Actual Work
*Week 3 | 3 lessons + 1 project*

### Lesson 3.1: The AI Task Audit
- How to identify which of your tasks AI can meaningfully help with
- The effort/quality matrix: when AI saves time vs. when it wastes it
- Tasks where AI should never be your first draft (and why)
- Exercise: map 10 of your regular tasks onto the matrix

### Lesson 3.2: Building Your First AI-Assisted Workflow
- What a workflow actually is (vs. a one-off prompt)
- How to chain prompts to handle complex, multi-step work
- Creating reusable prompt templates for your most common tasks

### Lesson 3.3: Editing AI Output — The Non-Negotiable Skill
- Why human editing is what makes AI output actually good
- The 5-point AI output review checklist:
  1. Factual accuracy
  2. Logical coherence
  3. Tone and voice match
  4. Completeness — what's missing?
  5. Your judgment — does this represent what you want to say?

**Week 3 Project:**
Design and use a complete AI-assisted workflow for one recurring task. Document the prompts, the output, and how you edited it. This becomes your workflow template.

## Module 4: Thinking Clearly About AI
*Week 4 | 3 lessons + final reflection*

### Lesson 4.1: The Dependency Trap — Keeping Your Own Thinking Sharp
- What cognitive offloading is and when it helps vs. hurts
- The skills that atrophy when you stop using them
- Designing your AI use to preserve, not erode, your judgment
- Neuroplasticity connection: how habits form around new tools

### Lesson 4.2: Evaluating AI Claims
- How to critically evaluate claims that "AI can do X"
- Reading AI benchmark results without being misled
- Questions to ask before adopting any new AI tool

### Lesson 4.3: Your AI Strategy — Intentional, Not Reactive
- From "using AI when I remember to" to "having a deliberate AI strategy"
- Revisiting your Week 1 task audit: what's changed?
- Setting up a monthly AI review practice

**Final Reflection:**
Write 300-500 words: What can you do now that you couldn't in Week 1? What surprised you? What will you do differently starting tomorrow?
