## Course Overview

**Tagline:** Your brain isn't fixed. Your learning strategies probably are. Let's fix that.

**Description:**
Most people learn the way they were taught in school: read, highlight, review, repeat. Decades of neuroscience say this is one of the least effective approaches to building durable knowledge. This course replaces it with strategies that work with your brain's actual biology — grounded in neuroplasticity research and built for adults with real demands on their time.

You'll learn how memory actually forms, why most learning strategies fail, and how to build a practice that makes new skills stick — whether you're learning AI tools, a new professional domain, or anything else that matters to you.

**Outcomes — by the end of this course, you will:**
- Understand how neuroplasticity works and what it means for adult learning
- Identify which of your current learning habits are working against you (and why)
- Apply 5 evidence-based learning techniques that significantly outperform conventional study
- Design a personal learning practice that fits your schedule and cognitive style
- Use sleep, stress management, and physical movement as learning amplifiers — not afterthoughts
- Evaluate any new learning tool or technique with a working model of how your brain actually changes

## Module 1: Your Brain Is Not a Hard Drive
*Week 1 | 3 lessons + 1 exercise*

### Lesson 1.1: What Neuroplasticity Actually Means
- The myth: your brain stops changing in your 20s
- The reality: synaptic plasticity across the lifespan
- What "rewiring" actually looks like at the neural level (no jargon required)
- Why this matters: your learning ability is not fixed

### Lesson 1.2: How Memory Forms (And Why Most of It Doesn't)
- Working memory vs. long-term memory — the real bottleneck
- Encoding, consolidation, retrieval — the three stages most learning skips
- The forgetting curve: why 70% of what you learn is gone within 24 hours without intervention
- What actually drives information into long-term memory

### Lesson 1.3: The Learning Strategies That Don't Work (And Why We Keep Using Them)
- Passive re-reading: why it creates familiarity without retention
- Highlighting and summarizing: the illusion of understanding
- Massed practice ("cramming"): short-term performance, long-term failure
- Why these strategies persist: they feel productive and are easy

**Week 1 Exercise:**
Pick something you tried to learn in the last 6 months that didn't stick. Map your approach against what you now know about how memory forms. What would you do differently?

## Module 2: The Techniques That Actually Work
*Week 2 | 4 lessons + 1 exercise*

### Lesson 2.1: Retrieval Practice — The Most Powerful Tool You're Probably Not Using
- The testing effect: retrieving information strengthens it more than re-studying it
- How to use retrieval practice without formal tests or flashcards
- The "write everything you remember" technique
- Why difficulty during practice is a sign it's working

### Lesson 2.2: Spaced Repetition — Fighting the Forgetting Curve Directly
- How spacing exposures over time exploits the forgetting curve
- The spacing effect: why studying the same material across 3 days beats 3 hours in one day
- Practical scheduling: how to space your learning without complicated systems
- When to use spaced repetition tools (Anki, Readwise) and when they're overkill

### Lesson 2.3: Interleaving — The Counterintuitive Practice That Accelerates Mastery
- Blocked vs. interleaved practice: what the research shows
- Why practicing multiple skills in a mixed order feels harder but produces better results
- How to design interleaved practice sessions for conceptual learning

### Lesson 2.4: Elaborative Interrogation and Self-Explanation
- Elaborative interrogation: asking "why?" and "how does this connect?" at every step
- Self-explanation: narrating your thinking as you work through material
- The generation effect: why producing information is stronger than receiving it

**Week 2 Exercise:**
Take one concept you're currently learning. Apply retrieval practice, then use elaborative interrogation to fill gaps.

## Module 3: The Biology of Learning Better
*Week 3 | 3 lessons + 1 project*

### Lesson 3.1: Sleep Is Not Optional — It's Where Learning Happens
- What happens in the brain during slow-wave sleep: replay and consolidation
- The research on sleep deprivation and memory: it's not subtle
- Practical sleep hygiene for learners: what actually moves the needle

### Lesson 3.2: Movement, BDNF, and the Exercise-Learning Connection
- BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor): what it is and why it matters
- The evidence on aerobic exercise and cognitive function / neurogenesis
- Timing: when to exercise relative to learning for maximum benefit

### Lesson 3.3: Stress, Cortisol, and the Learning Window
- How acute stress can enhance encoding — and how chronic stress destroys it
- The Yerkes-Dodson curve: optimal arousal for learning
- Designing low-threat, high-challenge learning conditions

**Week 3 Project:**
For 5 days, track: hours of sleep, any exercise, learning session length, and a subjective retention score (1-5) the next day.

## Module 4: Building Your Learning Practice
*Week 4 | 3 lessons + capstone*

### Lesson 4.1: Designing Your Personal Learning System
- The three components: intent, method, review
- The "one hour, three times a week" framework
- Matching learning methods to content types

### Lesson 4.2: Metacognition — Knowing What You Know (And Don't)
- The Dunning-Kruger effect in learning
- Calibration: how to develop accurate self-assessment
- Self-testing as the most reliable calibration tool

### Lesson 4.3: Staying a Learner — Identity, Motivation, and the Long Game
- Why "learning mode" is a mindset, not just a set of techniques
- Fixed vs. growth mindset: what the research actually says
- Building learning into identity rather than keeping it contingent on outcomes

**Capstone:**
Design your learning practice for the next 90 days: what you're learning, which techniques, your weekly schedule, and one review checkpoint.
