## Course Overview

**Tagline:** Your brain is an organ. Treat it like one.

**Description:**
Most of us take better care of our phones than our brains. We charge the phone every night, keep it updated, protect it from damage — then run our own minds on four hours of sleep, skipped meals, and constant stress, and wonder why focus and memory feel worse than they used to.

Brain Health 101 fixes that. It's a practical, evidence-based course on the five everyday levers that actually shape how well your brain works — today and decades from now: sleep, movement, nutrition, stress, and connection. No supplements to buy, no brain-training games to grind. Just the science of what keeps a brain sharp, and a realistic plan to build it into an ordinary week.

This is the health companion to our other courses: where *Neuroplasticity in Practice* teaches you to learn and rewire, Brain Health 101 keeps the organ doing the rewiring in good working order.

No medical background required. Nothing here replaces care from your doctor.

**Outcomes — by the end of this course, you will:**
- Explain what "brain health" actually means at the level of the organ — blood flow, neurons, and daily maintenance
- Identify the five levers that most affect your cognitive function, and how they interact
- Build a personalized sleep, movement, and nutrition routine that protects focus and memory
- Recognize the difference between evidence-based brain-health practices and marketing myths
- Assemble a sustainable weekly "brain maintenance" plan you can actually keep

**Duration:** 4 weeks | ~2 hours per week (8 hours total)
**Format:** Written lessons + exercises (video versions optional, per the Course 1 pattern)
**Level:** Beginner — no medical or scientific background required
**Price (proposed):** Rs 2,999 / $35 (one-time, lifetime access) | Early access: Rs 1,999 / $25 — mirrors Course 1 (product 120). Final price is Manish's decision; a WooCommerce product will mirror product 120 at publish time.

**Who this is for:** Anyone who feels their focus, memory, or mental energy slipping and wants to do something evidence-based about it — students, knowledge workers, parents, and anyone thinking ahead about cognitive longevity.

## Module 1: What Brain Health Actually Is
*Week 1 | 3 lessons + 1 exercise*

### Lesson 1.1: Your Brain Is an Organ — And You Can Change Its Health
- The brain as a living, metabolically hungry organ (2% of body weight, ~20% of energy use)
- What neurons, blood flow, and glial "maintenance" cells do day to day
- Why "brain health" is a set of daily inputs, not a fixed trait you're born with
- The single most important idea of the course: your habits are your brain's operating conditions

### Lesson 1.2: The Five Levers of Brain Health
- The five inputs the evidence keeps pointing to: sleep, movement, nutrition, stress, connection
- Why they work as a system, not a checklist — each one amplifies or undermines the others
- Where each lever shows up later in the course (and in our brain-health blog posts)
- The 80/20 of brain health: get these five roughly right before optimizing anything else

### Lesson 1.3: Myths, Marketing, and What Actually Works
- The "you only use 10% of your brain" myth, and why it won't die
- Brain-training games, nootropic supplements, and other tempting shortcuts — what the evidence really shows
- How to read a brain-health claim without getting fooled (correlation vs. cause, tiny studies, supplement marketing)
- The honest bottom line: the boring fundamentals beat the expensive shortcuts

**Week 1 Exercise:**
Score yourself 1–5 on each of the five levers (sleep, movement, nutrition, stress, connection). Pick the *lowest* one — that's your highest-leverage target for the rest of the course. Write one sentence on what "a 4" would look like for you on that lever.

## Module 2: Sleep — The Brain's Maintenance System
*Week 2 | 3 lessons + 1 exercise*

### Lesson 2.1: What Your Brain Does While You Sleep
- The glymphatic "wash cycle": clearing metabolic waste during deep sleep
- Memory consolidation and emotional processing across the night
- Why lost sleep is not something you can fully "catch up" on

### Lesson 2.2: Building Sleep That Actually Restores
- Consistency over duration: the power of a fixed wake time
- Light, caffeine, alcohol, and screens — the four biggest sleep saboteurs
- A realistic wind-down routine for real lives

### Lesson 2.3: When Sleep Won't Come — Troubleshooting Without Panic
- The difference between a bad night and a real problem
- Why "trying harder to sleep" backfires, and what to do instead
- When to see a professional (apnea, insomnia, and red flags)

**Week 2 Exercise:** Track your wake time (not bedtime) for seven days and hold it within a 30-minute window. Note your afternoon energy each day.
*Go deeper: blog post "How Sleep Rewires Your Brain" (post 68).*

## Module 3: Fuel and Movement — The Body-Brain Connection
*Week 3 | 3 lessons + 1 project*

### Lesson 3.1: How Movement Changes the Brain
- Exercise, blood flow, and BDNF ("fertilizer for the brain")
- Why even a daily walk moves the needle on mood and focus
- Strength, cardio, and the "exercise snack" for busy weeks

### Lesson 3.2: What You Eat and How You Think
- The gut-brain axis and steady blood sugar
- The handful of dietary patterns with real cognitive evidence (and the hype to ignore)
- Hydration, omega-3s, and the simple plate that supports a brain

### Lesson 3.3: Putting the Body-Brain Habits Together
- Stacking movement and food onto habits you already have
- The minimum effective dose: what to do on your worst, busiest week
- Building a routine you'll still be doing in three months

**Week 3 Project:** Design a one-week brain-fuel-and-movement plan using the minimum effective dose. Run it for a week.
*Go deeper: blog posts "Exercise Rewires Your Brain" (post 90) and "What You Eat Changes How You Think" (post 96).*

## Module 4: Stress, Mood, Connection & Cognitive Longevity
*Week 4 | 3 lessons + 1 capstone*

### Lesson 4.1: Stress and the Brain — Friend and Foe
- Acute vs. chronic stress, cortisol, and the hippocampus
- Why chronic stress quietly erodes learning and memory
- Practical, evidence-based buffers: breath, boundaries, and recovery

### Lesson 4.2: Mood, Connection, and the Social Brain
- Loneliness as a genuine cognitive risk factor
- How connection and purpose protect the aging brain
- Small social habits with outsized returns

### Lesson 4.3: The Long Game — Cognitive Reserve and Lifelong Brain Health
- What "cognitive reserve" is and how you build it
- Lifelong learning, novelty, and staying engaged
- Your sustainable weekly brain-maintenance plan

**Week 4 Capstone:** Combine your best habit from each of the four modules into a single one-page weekly brain-maintenance plan. This is what you leave the course with.
*Go deeper: blog post "Chronic Stress Is Quietly Wrecking Your Ability to Learn" (post 83).*
